Hello! I am a student of Epicodus a vocational school focused for web development. I started on July 31st, 2017 on the "Ruby on Rails" track. I'm super excited to be able to attend and I'm looking forward to learn as much as I can!

A few months ago, I was editing sound files for an addon as a request. With my background in audio engineering, I created a few sound effects, and then modified the .lua files -- when it kind of hit me that I wanted to learn more, and maybe even make my own addons!

After finishing that project, I sent the author the files and to my suprise, he pushed them to the master!

I started researching programming, and scraped the surface of python and terminal. I was hooked! I started making basic scripts, and started going out of my way to .touch files in terminal. However, I would often get stuck in a sort of "analysis paralysis" -- I was stuck in a loop of analyzing all the possible next steps, forgetting the most important thing when learning, everyone was a beginner once. I eventually stopped coding for a bit as everything felt just out of reach and a million miles away at the same time. I would spend so much time and energy researching what my next step should be that I never could fully commit to anything. It was terrible!

My solution? Just diving in! That's why I enrolled in Epicodus.
